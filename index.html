CHRISTINA LEE is a visual art studio using spatial frameworks and digital pipelines to expand human perception. Based across Malaysia and Taiwan, the studio focuses on how spatial boundaries can be altered to cultivate a deep sense of clarity and structural optimism.

The studio explores delicate loops of time, memory, and presence. By mapping raw artistic configurations directly against systematic media servers, we reconfigure traditional physical constraints into open environments.

Case Study / Primary Exhibition Spotlight

Between Me & The World: Situational Perception

Welcome to the other side where the mind is free to wander.

This exhibition invites the audiences to enter a world where the familiar stretches into the a whole new visual, and where all possibilities are laid bare.

Inspired by architectural spaces and landscapes, the visuals explore a balance of structure and spontaneity, encouraging curiosity and discovery.

Each work serves as a portal, guiding the audience through layers of reality, imagination, and introspection, encouraging them to see the world from new perspectives, challenging perceptions of what is real and what is possible.

Index Ref // 01 — Panoramic Configuration

DRIFT

Core Concept

The core concept focuses on the detachment and return of consciousness, and micro-disruptions within the everyday. It explores the "decoupling" of consciousness and body when the brain enters "auto-pilot" during routine actions like walking.

  • • Weightlessness: A psychological state where boundaries blur.
  • • Half-present: The sensory gap between body and mind.
  • • Micro-disruptions: Displacement of familiar objects outside of logic.

Visual Language

Weightlessness acts as the cause or psychological state, leading to a loss of direction. Drifting is the resulting behavior—a slow, aimless displacement driven by spatial flow; a process rather than a destination.

Technical Pipeline

4-sequence 1920x1080 / Resolume Arena / Mac mini / Epson Short-throw

Index Ref // 04 — 3D+VFX Series

Alien Interlude

A study of "half-presence" through non-linear movement and procedural character animation.

Philosophical Framework

This series explores weightlessness and the decoupling of the body from its environment. By introducing micro-disruptions in the animation cycle, the work highlights a state of being half-present within a digital void.

Technical Pipeline

• Procedural Scripting: Python-driven flight paths
• Animation: Blender Geometry Nodes
• Environment: Zero-G Simulation Canvas

Index Ref // 05 — System Automation

Data & Interactive Pipelines

Mapping systematic automation and algorithmic behaviors. This research focus utilizes scripting to bridge the gap between human perception and digital execution.

Structural Logic

Using Python to automate complex animation loops—such as bee flight paths and sequential architectural transitions—to create a seamless sense of weightlessness within digital environments.

Simulation Tools
